Explain how these bounce numbers relate to the particular effects that appear. Make sure to include all screenshots.

Max Ray Depth 0: Light source shows up as it is the only source of light without any bounces that can be seen.

Max Ray Depth 1: Walls appear as the light boucnes off the walls from the light source and into the camera. Shadows on spheres appear as we consider then as objects that block the light. Reflection of the light source shows up on the balls as the light from the source bounces off the sphere to the camera. This reflection is less bright on the glass ball as only some of it is reflected according to Schlick's approximation.

Max Ray Depth 2: Walls appear in the mirror ball as the light which bounced of the walls bounces off the mirror ball towards the camera. Light appears on the bottom of the glass ball as it has refracted through the ball as is ready to exit the ball onto the floor.

Max Ray Depth 3: Glass ball starts to look like a glass ball and shows the light from the top right of the room. This shows up as the light bounces of the top right wall, into the back of the glass ball and finally refracted towards the front of the ball with it's third bounce. Light shows up on the bottom of glass ball as the light refracted out of the bottom of the ball from before bounces off of the floor.

Max Ray Depth 4: Light appears on the blue wall which is quite faint. Most likely from the light striking the mirror ball, bouncing off into the glass ball, refracted within the glass ball, which finally strikes the blue wall.

Max Ray Depth 5, 100: Not many discernable features seem to appear outside of the fact that the light on top of the spere becomes brighter, this is most likely from the ray bouncing into the glass sphere, refracting towards the bottom of the sphere, striking the floor, reflecting back into the bottom of the sphere and then refracting back out of the top of the sphere.

For these subparts, we recommend using a microfacet BSDF scene to show off the cool out of focus effects you can get with depth of field!

In a few sentences, explain the differences between a pinhole camera model and a thin-lens camera model.

The largest difference between a pinhole camera and a thin lens model is that a pinhole model is able to focus light from any distance. Unlike the thin lens model which has a focal plane, a pinhole only has one small opening which only allows light to come through which means that everything will be in focus.
